## Summary

This Epoch AI report tracks the physical build-out of OpenAI's Stargate project across the United States. The main message is that Stargate is no longer a vague announcement: all seven sites now show active development, one site in Abilene is already partially operational, and the combined project is heading toward more than 9 GW of planned capacity by 2029.

## Core Thesis

- Stargate is a $500 billion AI infrastructure program involving OpenAI, Oracle, and SoftBank.
- The project now spans seven US locations.
- The Abilene, Texas site is the most advanced and already has roughly 0.6 GW online.
- The remaining sites are in Texas, New Mexico, Wisconsin, Michigan, and Ohio.
- The biggest constraints are power delivery, cooling, and local build-out logistics.

## Main Numbers

- Current operational capacity: about `0.6 GW`
- Planned total capacity: more than `9 GW`
- Estimated H100-equivalent compute at full build-out: about `20 million` GPUs' worth
- Comparison used by Epoch: roughly comparable to the peak power demand of New York City

## Why The Sites Matter

The article treats Stargate as a case study in how frontier AI infrastructure is actually getting built in the US. The site list shows the tradeoffs builders are making:

- use on-site natural gas where grid interconnection would be slow,
- use closed-loop liquid cooling where water use is politically sensitive,
- accept higher facility cost in exchange for speed and reliability,
- split ownership and infrastructure responsibilities across multiple companies.

## Site-by-Site Snapshot

### Abilene, Texas

- Current capacity: `0.6 GW`
- Projected capacity: `1.2 GW`
- Projected completion: `Q3 2026`

This is the flagship site. Epoch says several of the buildings are already operational and house Nvidia Blackwell chips. Power comes from a mix of on-site natural gas and grid power, including local wind.

OpenAI had originally planned a larger expansion here, but the report says that capacity was redirected to other locations.

### Shackelford County, Texas

- Current capacity: `0 GW`
- Projected capacity: `2.0 GW`
- Projected completion: `Q4 2028`

This site is being developed by Vantage on a large campus just across the county line from Abilene. It will use an on-site natural gas microgrid, and construction imagery shows early building progress.

### Dona Ana County, New Mexico

- Current capacity: `0 GW`
- Projected capacity: `2.2 GW`
- Projected completion: `Q4 2028`

STACK Infrastructure is developing Project Jupiter here. The report says foundation work is underway and that two natural gas microgrids are planned to limit grid impact.

### Milam County, Texas

- Current capacity: `0 GW`
- Projected capacity: `1.2 GW`
- Projected completion: `Q4 2028`

SoftBank subsidiary SB Energy is building and operating this site. Epoch describes it as a fast-build campus, with early steel framing and roofing visible in satellite imagery. The article says SB Energy plans to fund and build new energy generation and storage for most of the power demand.

### Port Washington, Wisconsin

- Current capacity: `0 GW`
- Projected capacity: `1.3 GW`
- Projected completion: `Q4 2028`

This Vantage campus, called Lighthouse, is positioned as sustainable-by-design. The article says 70% of power is expected to come from solar, wind, and battery storage.

### Saline Township, Michigan

- Current capacity: `0 GW`
- Projected capacity: `1.4 GW`
- Projected completion: `Q4 2028`

Related Digital is developing The Barn campus here. Epoch says foundation work is visible in satellite imagery, and that DTE Energy will provide all power, augmented by battery storage.

### Lordstown, Ohio

- Current capacity: `0 GW`
- Projected capacity: less than `0.3 GW`
- Projected completion: `Unknown`

This is the least specific site in the report. It is still in active development, but Epoch does not give a firm completion date.

## Design Choices Epoch Highlights

### Power

Stargate's developers are using a mix of grid power and onsite generation. The main reason is to avoid long waits for grid interconnection and to reduce risk around power availability.

### Cooling

The article says at least six of the seven sites are using closed-loop liquid cooling, which avoids evaporating water and helps address local concerns about water consumption.

### Ownership

Based on OpenAI announcements in 2025, SoftBank will own the hardware at Milam County and Ohio, while Oracle will own the hardware at the remaining sites. All of the sites will serve OpenAI workloads.
