## Why This Source Matters

Khan Academy is useful here as the "learner path" source: short lessons, unit tests, mastery skills, simulations, and worked examples. This repo should not copy that course. Instead, it should use Khan as a sanity check for the reading order, examples, and practice emphasis.

## Course Shape

The Khan map follows the same broad eight biology content units:

1. Chemistry of life
2. Cell structure and function
3. Cellular energetics
4. Cell communication and cell cycle
5. Heredity
6. Gene expression and regulation
7. Natural selection
8. Ecology

It also includes simulations, worked examples of free-response questions, and standards mappings. Those extras are useful for future practice files, but the first pass in this repo focuses on readable lessons.

## Pedagogical Signals To Reuse

- Divide long units into small concept chunks.
- Pair explanations with quick checks.
- Use concrete biological examples: water, membranes, enzymes, photosynthesis, respiration, cell-cycle checkpoints, pedigrees, Hardy-Weinberg, food webs, and biodiversity disruption.
- Treat diagrams and data as core biology, not decoration.
- Include mastery-style review prompts at the end of each lesson.

## Unit-Level Concepts Used Here

Chemistry of life:
- water's properties,
- elements and functional groups,
- macromolecule structure and function,
- nucleic acids.

Cells:
- organelles,
- cell size,
- plasma membranes,
- permeability,
- transport,
- tonicity,
- compartmentalization.

Cellular energetics:
- enzymes,
- pH and inhibition,
- thermodynamics and ATP,
- photosynthesis,
- respiration,
- molecular variation and fitness.

Communication and division:
- cell signaling modes,
- receptors and signal relay,
- feedback,
- cell cycle and checkpoints.

Heredity:
- meiosis,
- Mendelian inheritance,
- non-Mendelian inheritance,
- phenotype and environment,
- chromosomal inheritance.

Gene expression:
- DNA/RNA structure,
- replication,
- transcription,
- translation,
- gene regulation,
- mutation,
- biotechnology.

Evolution:
- natural selection,
- artificial selection,
- drift and population genetics,
- Hardy-Weinberg,
- evidence and common ancestry,
- phylogeny,
- speciation,
- variation,
- origins of life.

Ecology:
- behavior and responses,
- energy flow,
- population growth,
- communities,
- biodiversity,
- ecosystem disruption.

## Translation Into Local Lessons

Khan provides the modular rhythm; this repo provides the narrative. The local Biology lessons should feel like short textbook chapters that can be read in order, with enough examples to make the ideas stick without requiring a video.
