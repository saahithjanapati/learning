  - Extending RLVR beyond verifiable tasks: I’m also interested in extending RLVR beyond settings where there is a clean right answer. Building off of Scale’s work on rubrics, I think it would be interesting to study the limits of rubric-based methods: where they continue to work well, where they start to break down, and what additional ideas are needed in more open-ended domains where quality depends on judgment, taste, or creativity, and in longer-horizon tasks where evaluation needs to happen over full trajectories.

  - Agent monitoring/interface design: Another area I’ve been thinking about recently is how users should interact with agents as they start doing longer-running tasks. I think it would be interesting to study what kinds of interfaces between models and users make it possible to actually monitor what is happening, surface progress or uncertainty in a useful way, and let users steer or intervene when needed.
