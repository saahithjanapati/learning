## Problem 1.1

Suppose $X_1,\dots,X_8$ are ternary random variables, so each variable takes $3$ possible values.

1. How many entries does the full joint table $p(x_1,\dots,x_8)$ have?
2. Explain in one or two sentences why graphical models become attractive in this setting.

## Problem 1.2

For each item below, classify it as mainly `representation`, `inference`, or `learning / sampling`.

1. Write the joint distribution implied by a graph.
2. Compute $p(x_2 \mid x_5)$.
3. Approximate a posterior by optimization.
4. Draw samples from a model.

## Problem 1.3

In your own words, explain the difference between:

1. the graph
2. the factorization
3. the full joint distribution

Your answer should make clear why these are related but not identical objects.

## Problem 1.4

Why does a compact representation of a probabilistic model not automatically guarantee that exact inference is easy?

Answer in 3 to 5 sentences.
