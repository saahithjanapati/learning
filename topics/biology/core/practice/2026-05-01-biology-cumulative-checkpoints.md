# Biology Core Cumulative Checkpoints

Use these after reading the Biology Core lessons. They are designed for self-checking, not grading.

## How To Use

Pick one unit, answer without notes, then reread only the section that felt shaky. If an answer feels vague, force it into this pattern:

claim -> evidence or mechanism -> consequence

## Unit 1: Chemistry Of Life

1. Explain why water's polarity matters for cells.
2. Compare carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids by structure and function.
3. Give one example where a small structural difference changes a molecule's biological role.
4. Explain dehydration synthesis and hydrolysis without using the words "build" or "break."

## Unit 2: Cells And Membranes

1. Why does surface area-to-volume ratio limit cell size?
2. Predict what happens to an animal cell in hypotonic, isotonic, and hypertonic solutions.
3. Compare simple diffusion, facilitated diffusion, primary active transport, and secondary active transport.
4. Explain how the endosymbiotic theory connects cell structure to evolution.

## Unit 3: Cellular Energetics

1. Explain activation energy and why enzymes matter.
2. Trace the role of a proton gradient in ATP production.
3. Compare photosynthesis and cellular respiration as energy transformations.
4. Explain why fermentation is useful even though it produces less ATP than aerobic respiration.

## Unit 4: Communication And Division

1. Trace a signal from ligand binding to cellular response.
2. Compare endocrine, paracrine, synaptic, autocrine, and direct-contact signaling.
3. Explain negative feedback and positive feedback using one biological example each.
4. Describe why checkpoints reduce the risk of harmful cell division.

## Unit 5: Heredity

1. Explain how meiosis produces genetic diversity.
2. Distinguish genotype from phenotype using a concrete example.
3. Explain why linked genes violate the simplest independent-assortment expectation.
4. Give one example where environment changes phenotype without changing genotype.

## Unit 6: Gene Expression And Biotechnology

1. Trace DNA -> RNA -> protein in order.
2. Explain why gene regulation is necessary for cell specialization.
3. Compare silent, missense, and nonsense mutations.
4. Explain what PCR, gel electrophoresis, sequencing, and genetic engineering are each useful for.

## Unit 7: Evolution

1. Explain why populations evolve but individuals do not.
2. Compare natural selection, genetic drift, gene flow, and mutation.
3. Use Hardy-Weinberg as a null model: what assumptions must hold?
4. Explain how common ancestry can be inferred from multiple independent evidence types.

## Unit 8: Ecology

1. Explain why energy flow and matter cycling are different.
2. Compare exponential and logistic population growth.
3. Give an example of an indirect effect in a food web.
4. Explain why biodiversity can make ecosystems more resilient.

## Cumulative Synthesis Prompts

1. Start with a mutation and trace a possible path to a changed protein, changed phenotype, changed fitness, and changed allele frequency.
2. Start with sunlight and trace a possible path to ATP in a herbivore muscle cell.
3. Start with a membrane protein and explain how it could matter for cell transport, organism survival, and evolution.
4. Start with a habitat disruption and trace possible effects on behavior, population size, community interactions, and biodiversity.

## One-Page Capstone

Write a one-page explanation of biology using these four words as section anchors:

- energy,
- information,
- structure,
- interaction.

The goal is not to include every fact. The goal is to show that the facts now live inside one connected map.
