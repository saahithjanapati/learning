## Starting Assumption

You took biology before and do not need to be treated like a total beginner, but you want the ideas reassembled into a coherent story that is easy to read in small sessions. The goal is not exam cramming. The goal is to rebuild a durable biology map that can later expand into deeper molecular biology, physiology, ecology, evolution, genomics, and biochemistry.

## Target Outcome (What "1" Means)

- [ ] Can explain how molecular structure controls biological function.
- [ ] Can connect cell structure, membranes, energy, communication, and division.
- [ ] Can reason through heredity, gene expression, mutation, and biotechnology.
- [ ] Can explain evolution as population-level change over generations.
- [ ] Can interpret ecology as interacting systems of energy, matter, organisms, and disturbance.
- [ ] Can read simple biological data, diagrams, experimental setups, and claims without freezing.

## Unifying Story

Biology is the study of living systems that maintain order, process energy, store information, reproduce, evolve, and interact. This course follows that arc:

1. Life is built from matter with special chemical properties.
2. Cells use boundaries and compartments to control chemistry.
3. Energy flow powers order, growth, repair, and reproduction.
4. Cells sense signals and decide what to do next.
5. Inheritance transmits biological information across generations.
6. Gene expression turns stored information into cellular action.
7. Evolution changes populations and explains unity plus diversity.
8. Ecology scales those interactions up to populations, communities, and ecosystems.

## Modules

1. Course map and science practices
- Objective: see the whole field before zooming into details.
- Content type: exposition
- Exit check: explain the course in one paragraph using the words energy, information, interaction, and evolution.
- Lesson: [Biology course overview](../lessons/2026-05-01-biology-course-overview.md)
- Lesson: [Science practices toolkit](../lessons/2026-05-01-biology-science-practices-toolkit.md)

2. Chemistry of life
- Objective: understand why water, carbon, and macromolecules make life possible.
- Content type: exposition with quick checks
- Exit check: connect one molecule's structure to its function.
- Lesson: [Chemistry of life](../lessons/2026-05-01-biology-01-chemistry-of-life.md)

3. Cells and membranes
- Objective: understand cells as bounded, organized, selectively permeable systems.
- Content type: exposition with diagram reasoning
- Exit check: predict how a cell responds to a concentration gradient.
- Lesson: [Cells and membranes](../lessons/2026-05-01-biology-02-cells-and-membranes.md)

4. Cellular energetics
- Objective: explain enzymes, ATP, photosynthesis, respiration, and energy transformations.
- Content type: exposition with mechanism tracing
- Exit check: trace energy from sunlight or food into ATP.
- Lesson: [Cellular energetics](../lessons/2026-05-01-biology-03-cellular-energetics.md)

5. Cell communication and division
- Objective: understand signaling, feedback, cell-cycle control, and why regulation matters.
- Content type: exposition with cause-effect checks
- Exit check: explain how one signal can cause different responses in different cell types.
- Lesson: [Cell communication and division](../lessons/2026-05-01-biology-04-cell-communication-and-division.md)

6. Heredity
- Objective: connect meiosis, probability, chromosomes, and phenotype.
- Content type: exposition with small genetics examples
- Exit check: explain why siblings are similar but not genetically identical.
- Lesson: [Heredity](../lessons/2026-05-01-biology-05-heredity.md)

7. Gene expression and biotechnology
- Objective: explain how DNA information is copied, read, regulated, changed, and engineered.
- Content type: exposition with process diagrams in prose
- Exit check: trace DNA -> RNA -> protein and name where regulation can occur.
- Lesson: [Gene expression and biotechnology](../lessons/2026-05-01-biology-06-gene-expression-and-biotechnology.md)

8. Evolution and natural selection
- Objective: explain evolution through variation, inheritance, selection, drift, gene flow, and time.
- Content type: exposition with data intuition
- Exit check: distinguish natural selection from genetic drift in a population story.
- Lesson: [Evolution and natural selection](../lessons/2026-05-01-biology-07-evolution-and-natural-selection.md)

9. Ecology
- Objective: reason about organisms in systems: behavior, populations, communities, ecosystems, and disruption.
- Content type: exposition with systems thinking
- Exit check: explain how one disturbance can move through a food web.
- Lesson: [Ecology](../lessons/2026-05-01-biology-08-ecology.md)

## Session Cadence

- Session length: 15-35 minutes for casual reading; 45-75 minutes if paired with practice.
- Sessions per week: flexible, 2-5 short readings works well.
- Practice ratio progression:
  - early: 80% reading, 20% self-checks
  - middle: 60% reading, 40% diagrams/data/explanations
  - late: 50% reading, 50% cumulative recall and mixed prompts

## Assessment Plan

- Quick checks: answer each lesson's final prompts without rereading.
- Medium practice: make one concept map per unit connecting vocabulary to mechanism.
- Data practice: interpret one graph, experimental setup, or model per module.
- Capstone task: write a two-page "biology from molecules to ecosystems" explanation that connects all eight content modules into one story.
