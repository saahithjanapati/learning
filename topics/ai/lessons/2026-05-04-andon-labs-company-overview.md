## The One-Sentence Version

Andon Labs is building AI evaluation and autonomy-testing infrastructure around the idea that future powerful agents cannot rely on humans staying meaningfully in the loop.

## What Andon Labs Says It Does

The site emphasizes custom evaluations, publications, and real-world autonomy benchmarks such as vending, radio-station, and robotics-style tasks.

## Its Safety Thesis

The company takes a strong view: human-in-the-loop safety will eventually be too weak if agents become capable enough to operate at organizational scale. So the problem becomes how to benchmark, control, and safely structure autonomous systems before that transition happens.

## Why Real-World Benchmarks Matter Here

Andon Labs appears to care less about toy evals and more about messy, operational tasks that resemble businesses or long-horizon systems. That makes its work relevant to the broader shift from benchmark QA toward autonomous-agent evaluation.

## Why The Company Is Interesting

The company is interesting because it combines AI safety language with very operational deployment-style benchmarking. It is trying to study control and autonomy in settings where "the agent runs a real process" is the actual unit of analysis.
