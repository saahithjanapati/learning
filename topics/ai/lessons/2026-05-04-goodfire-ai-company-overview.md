## The One-Sentence Version

Goodfire is an interpretability-focused AI lab trying to turn understanding neural-network internals into practical tools for debugging and intentionally designing models.

## What Goodfire Says It Is Building

The company frames itself around moving AI development from `alchemy` to `precision engineering`. That means better ways to inspect internal representations, validate what a model has actually learned, and intervene more deliberately during development.

## Why Interpretability Is The Center

Goodfire's pitch is that modern model training is powerful but still too opaque. If internal structure stays mysterious, developers are forced to rely too heavily on external behavior alone.

## Silico And The Product Direction

The site presents `Silico` as a platform for understanding and debugging models. That suggests the company is not only publishing interpretability research, but trying to package it into a product layer for model builders.

## Why The Company Matters

Goodfire is interesting because it treats interpretability as a development primitive rather than a side research curiosity. If that works, it changes how teams debug, steer, and trust advanced models.
